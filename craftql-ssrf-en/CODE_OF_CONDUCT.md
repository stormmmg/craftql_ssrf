## ‚öñÔ∏è Legal Compliance

### Understanding the Law

Users of this POC must understand and comply with:

#### United States Laws

- **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)**
  - 18 U.S.C. ¬ß 1030
  - Prohibits unauthorized access to computer systems
  - Penalties: Fines and imprisonment

- **Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)**
  - 17 U.S.C. ¬ß 1201
  - Prohibits circumvention of security measures
  - Exceptions for security testing (Section 1201(j))

#### International Laws

- **EU Convention on Cybercrime**
- **UK Computer Misuse Act**
- **Australia Cybercrime Act**
- **Other local jurisdictions**

### Authorization Requirements

**Always obtain explicit written authorization before:**

- Testing any system you don't own
- Running POCs against production environments
- Accessing or downloading sensitive data
- Conducting port scans or vulnerability assessments

**Proper authorization includes:**

- Written Rules of Engagement (ROE)
- Defined testing scope and duration
- Point of contact information
- Incident response procedures

## ü§ù Community Standards

### Professional Conduct

1. **Respect**
   - Treat all community members with respect
   - Acknowledge the work of security researchers
   - Collaborate constructively

2. **Transparency**
   - Disclose vulnerabilities responsibly
   - Provide accurate information
   - Share remediation guidance

3. **Accountability**
   - Take responsibility for your actions
   - Report misuse of this POC

### Reporting Misuse

If you become aware of misuse of this POC:

1. **Document the incident**
   - Date, time, and details
   - Evidence of unauthorized use
   - Impact assessment

2. **Report to appropriate authorities**
   - Local law enforcement
   - Victim organization's security team
   - Project maintainers

3. **Cooperate with investigations**
   - Provide requested information
   - Assist in remediation efforts
   - Support prosecution of offenders
